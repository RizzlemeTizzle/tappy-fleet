# Tappy Website Brief: Add Fleet Management for Business

## Goal

Update the Tappy website so it clearly markets two audiences:

- `Tappy for Private`: individual drivers who want simple charging access and convenience.
- `Tappy for Business`: companies that need fleet charging management, billing control, and operational visibility.

The website should make it obvious that Tappy is no longer only a consumer product. It should also present Tappy as a fleet management platform for employers, operations teams, and finance teams.

## Recommended Website Structure

Use a visible switch, tab group, or segmented control near the top of the homepage:

- `Private`
- `Business`

This is the recommended direction because it lets both audiences self-select quickly without forcing one mixed message. It also avoids burying the business offer under consumer-oriented copy.

If a switch is added, keep shared brand elements consistent, but change the messaging, feature blocks, proof points, and CTA based on the selected audience.

## Recommended Navigation / Information Architecture

### Option A: Best option

Keep one homepage with a `Private / Business` toggle, then link to dedicated sections or landing pages:

- `/` or homepage with toggle
- `/business` or business landing page for Tappy Fleet
- `/private` or private-driver landing page if needed

### Option B: Good fallback

Keep the current homepage but add a strong business section lower on the page with a CTA such as:

- `Manage your company fleet`
- `Explore Tappy for Business`
- `Book a demo`

Option A is better for marketing because it gives each audience focused messaging and cleaner CTAs.

## Business Features That Should Be Marketed

These points should be based on the actual product capabilities in `tappy-fleet`.

### 1. Multi-organization fleet workspace

Tappy Fleet supports:

- Managing multiple organizations
- Switching between organizations from one workspace
- Editing company details such as legal name, billing email, VAT number, address, and payment mode

Marketing angle:

- Ideal for companies operating multiple entities, regions, or business units

Suggested copy:

> Manage one or multiple company fleets from a single workspace.

### 2. Employee and member management

Tappy Fleet supports:

- Inviting employees by email
- Assigning roles
- Viewing member status
- Suspending or reactivating access
- Removing members

Available roles visible in the product include:

- `EMPLOYEE`
- `TEAM_MANAGER`
- `FLEET_ADMIN`
- `FINANCE_ADMIN`
- `FLEET_OWNER`

Marketing angle:

- Centralized access control for company charging programs

Suggested copy:

> Invite employees, assign permissions, and control access from one dashboard.

### 3. Charging policies and spend rules

Tappy Fleet supports policy controls such as:

- Maximum spend per charging session
- Maximum monthly spend
- Allowed charging hours
- Business-days-only charging
- AC-only charging restrictions

Marketing angle:

- Turn charging into a governed company benefit instead of an uncontrolled expense

Suggested copy:

> Create charging policies that match your company budget and usage rules.

### 4. Billing and invoice management

Tappy Fleet supports:

- Automatic invoice generation
- Monthly billing periods
- Invoice statuses
- PDF invoice downloads
- Different payment modes, including company-paid and reimbursable flows

Marketing angle:

- Reduce finance overhead and make charging costs easier to manage

Suggested copy:

> Automate monthly charging invoices and keep finance in control.

### 5. Reporting and exports

Tappy Fleet supports:

- Session reporting
- Date filtering
- CSV export
- Usage visibility across employees, stations, dates, kWh, and cost
- Billing-mode visibility per session

Marketing angle:

- Give fleet and finance teams clean data for reconciliation and decision-making

Suggested copy:

> Track charging activity, monitor costs, and export reports when you need them.

### 6. Audit log and accountability

Tappy Fleet supports:

- Audit log visibility for fleet actions
- Timestamped events
- Action and entity history

Marketing angle:

- Useful for internal controls, admin visibility, and governance

Suggested copy:

> Keep a clear audit trail of key actions across your fleet workspace.

## Suggested Business Landing Page Sections

### 1. Hero

Headline ideas:

- `Charging management for modern EV fleets`
- `One platform for company charging control`
- `Manage employee charging without spreadsheet chaos`

Supporting copy:

> Tappy Fleet helps companies manage employee charging access, policy rules, billing, and reporting in one place.

Primary CTA ideas:

- `Book a demo`
- `Talk to sales`
- `Explore Tappy Fleet`

Secondary CTA ideas:

- `See fleet features`
- `How it works`

### 2. Problem / value section

Explain the pain clearly:

- Company charging costs are hard to track
- Employee access is difficult to manage manually
- Finance teams need invoices and exports
- Businesses need policy controls, not just charging access

### 3. Feature grid

Use 4 to 6 cards:

- Employee management
- Charging policies
- Billing and invoices
- Reports and exports
- Multi-organization workspace
- Audit trail

### 4. Role-based value section

This is important for business buyers. Split value by audience:

- `Fleet managers`: oversee usage and employee access
- `Finance teams`: track costs, invoices, and reimbursement flows
- `Operations / HR`: onboard and manage employees

### 5. How it works

Suggested flow:

1. Create your organization
2. Invite employees and assign roles
3. Set charging policies and payment mode
4. Monitor sessions, invoices, and reports

### 6. CTA section

Close with a clear business CTA:

> Ready to manage EV charging across your company?

CTA:

- `Request a demo`
- `Contact sales`

## Messaging Notes

Use business language that speaks to outcomes, not only features:

- `Control charging costs`
- `Manage employee access`
- `Reduce admin work`
- `Automate invoices`
- `Export fleet reports`
- `Set policy guardrails`

Avoid positioning the business offer as just a larger version of the consumer product. It should feel like a proper admin platform for companies.

## Visual / UX Notes

For the business version of the site:

- Show dashboard-style product visuals if available
- Use screenshots or mockups of fleet overview, employees, policies, reports, or billing
- Emphasize structure, control, and trust

For the private version:

- Keep the tone lighter and more consumer-oriented
- Focus on ease, speed, and convenience

The two experiences should feel related, but not identical.

## CTA Recommendations

For `Private`:

- `Get started`
- `Start charging`

For `Business`:

- `Book a demo`
- `Talk to sales`
- `Explore fleet features`

## Short Summary for the Website Team

Tappy should now be marketed as two clear offers:

- A consumer charging experience for private users
- A fleet management platform for companies

The business side should highlight these real product capabilities:

- Organization management
- Employee invites and access control
- Role-based administration
- Charging policies and spend limits
- Monthly invoices and PDF downloads
- Session reports and CSV exports
- Audit logs

The recommended UX direction is a homepage `Private / Business` switch plus a dedicated business landing page or section for Tappy Fleet.
